Ng'ombe Crew

Code of Policies & Operational Guidelines

1. Purpose and Scope

This policy framework sets the standard of conduct, safety, administration, and representation for all members, leaders, and volunteers of Ng'ombe Crew. It aligns directly with the Constitution and national policies of the Kenya Scouts Association (KSA).

2. Safe from Harm & Safeguarding Policy

Aligned with KSA Safe from Harm Policy & Child Protection Policy

  • ● Zero Tolerance: Physical abuse, verbal harassment, bullying, discrimination, or exploitation of any kind is strictly prohibited within or outside Crew activities.
  • ● Adult & Rover Boundaries: Rover Scouts and adult leaders must always maintain appropriate physical and emotional boundaries when interacting with younger youth or junior Scouts.
  • ● Incident Reporting: Any safeguarding concern, breach, or threat to member safety must be reported immediately to the Crew Leader or designated Safeguarding Officer for escalation according to KSA channels.

3. Youth Programme & Advancement Policy

Aligned with KSA Youth Programme Policy

  • ● Personal Growth: All members are expected to actively participate in the Rover Scout programme, advancement badges, community service projects, and skill-building activities.
  • ● Patrol/Crew System: Activities will be driven by democratic decision-making, youth leadership, and active team participation.

4. Uniform, Brand & Public Representation Policy

Aligned with KSA Brand Policy & Public Relations/Media Guidelines

  • ● Official Uniform: Members must wear the official KSA uniform and Ng'ombe Crew identity correctly and proudly during all official meetings, camps, and public events.
  • ● Media & Socials: Members must obtain approval from the Crew Council before publishing photos, videos, or official statements representing Ng'ombe Crew or KSA on public platforms.
  • ● Public Conduct: Members serve as ambassadors of the Movement and must adhere to the Scout Promise and Law at all times in public settings.

5. Safety, Security & Field Risk Management

Aligned with KSA Security & Special Programmes Policy

  • ● Risk Assessment: A mandatory risk assessment, safety briefing, and emergency plan must be conducted prior to any outdoor activity, camp, or hike.
  • ● Substance Abuse: Alcohol, illegal drugs, and smoking are strictly forbidden during any Crew activity, campsite, or while wearing the Scout uniform.

6. Adults & Volunteers in Scouting Policy

Aligned with KSA Adults in Scouting & Volunteers Policy

  • ● Vetting & Onboarding: All adult advisors, patrons, and volunteer leaders joining Ng'ombe Crew must undergo proper KSA registration, background checks, and orientation.
  • ● Continuous Training: Crew leaders and advisors are required to undertake KSA Wood Badge/ITC training courses to maintain high standards of adult support.

7. Governance, Resources & Financial Integrity

Aligned with KSA Procurement & Resource Mobilization Policies

  • ● Dues & Contributions: Subscriptions, project funds, and gear purchases must be accurately recorded, receipted, and managed by the Crew Treasurer.
  • ● Transparency: Crew funds and equipment are collective assets intended strictly for Scouting purposes; unauthorized personal use is forbidden.
  • ● Resource Management: All equipment belonging to Ng'ombe Crew must be properly maintained, cleaned, and inventoried after every event.

8. Commitment & Declaration

By joining Ng'ombe Crew, every member pledges to uphold these policies, live by the Scout Promise and Law, and actively contribute to creating a safe, positive, and progressive environment for all.
